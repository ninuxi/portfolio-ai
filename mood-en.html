How it works

Three steps, one unique experience

The creativity is yours: the content, the atmospheres, the artistic vision. MOOD analyses what happens in the space and autonomously decides which atmosphere to activate. Over time, it learns to do it better and better.

Step 01

Sees and listens

Non-invasive cameras and microphones read the room's energy: how many people there are, how they move, whether they talk or stay silent, how much noise there is. Data is processed anonymously — MOOD reads the collective atmosphere, not the individuals.

Step 02

Interprets

The AI analyses the signals and identifies the type of atmosphere underway: is the room silent, are people talking loudly, is someone singing, others laughing, is there dancing and stillness, people chatting and drinking? With each analysis cycle, MOOD updates its reading and assigns a state (contemplative, social, energetic, mysterious, and others).

MOOD learns over time: every session becomes training. The more it works, the more precise it becomes for that specific space.
Step 03

Orchestrates

MOOD automatically sends commands to the software controlling audio, video and lights — all within 200ms of the reading. The content is programmed by you: playlists, lighting scenes, visuals. MOOD decides when and which atmosphere to activate, in coherence with the moment.

Integrations

Works with the tools you already use

Software like QLab, Resolume, TouchDesigner or lighting consoles are programmed by you: create audio cues, design video scenes, configure the lights. That is your creativity.

MOOD doesn't create the content — it decides when to activate it. It manages the playlists and libraries you've already prepared, chooses the right scene based on what it senses in the space, and coordinates all systems together.

If the location already has screens, speakers, lights, projectors and a control system, MOOD integrates without replacing anything. If starting from scratch, we design everything together.

Compatible with: Chamsys MagicQ · grandMA3 · Ableton Live · Millumin · any OSC / ArtNet / MIDI system

MOOD is not an immersive installation. It doesn't project on giant screens and doesn't replace art. It's the system that creates the atmosphere around the art, the event, the space — and changes with those who experience it.
